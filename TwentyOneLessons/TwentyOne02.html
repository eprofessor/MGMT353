Exercise 1-Chapters 5 to 9

We will discuss this material during class.

Technological advances are changing our world at a rate never before experienced.

The twin revolutions in information technology and biotechnology confront us with the biggest challenges our species has ever encountered.
The merger of infotech and biotech threatens the core modern values of liberty and equality.
Any solution to the technological challenge has to involve global cooperation.
But nationalism, religion and culture divide humankind into hostile camps and make it very difficult to cooperate on a global level.

5. (50) Community

Human evolution and physiology has placed a limit of approximatey 150 meaningful relationships on an individual.
Building communities takes time and effort by all parties involved.
Technology is allowing the creation of massive online communities.
Reflect on the merging of online and offline communities.


What safeguards, if any, are needed, to prevent the manipulation of our entire bodies in a way that protects individual liberty while maintaining robust offline community?

6. (50) Civilization

Humans of any era tend to re-write history to their current mood.
Several examples are noted in this chapter.
The current global political paradigm is based on the accepted principles of human rights, state sovereignty, and international law.
This situation has resulted in world stability, best epitomized by the universal veneration of the American dollar.
Science and medicine has become more trusted for answers to problems.
Today we all participate in a global civilization, but there are still disagreements.


How many civilizations currently exist on our planet?

7. (50) Nationalism

The current threats to human civilization-nuclear war, ecological collapse and technological disruption-pose an existential crisis.
Worse yet, their interdependencies may create a whole other set of problems.
Nationalism, and returning to the world of the past, does not hold the answer.


What is needed to resolve our current set of unprecedented global predicaments?

8. (50) Religion

In the past thousand years the focus of religion has shifted from solving problems to providing interpretations and justifying positions.
Science now provides us with answers to technical questions, such as curing diseases like measles.
The science of economics provides the same answers for all religions.
Religion can still effectively unite a country, witness the changes in Japan from 1853 to 1941.


What role can religion play today in solving the world's probems?

9. (50) Immigration

Differences between current societies such as England and India are far less than they were one thousand years ago.
But cultural differences still exist.
People have been moving to better themselves .
Issues of assimilation, tolerance, changes to the national identity all bear on the resolution of the problems.
Racism can easily be mistaken for culturism.


What is the current situation with regard to immigration and assimilation of refugees in Europe?
